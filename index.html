Automessage Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 25, 2026

Automessage is a Chrome extension that provides AI-generated reply suggestions for Gmail threads. This policy explains what information the extension processes and how that information is used.

Information Automessage Processes

Automessage processes email content only when you use the extension on an open Gmail thread. To generate reply suggestions, the extension may read the current thread subject, the most recent message body, the Gmail thread identifier, and sender details if they are available in the Gmail page.

How Information Is Used

The information is used only to generate AI reply suggestions. The extension does not automatically send emails, does not read your inbox in bulk, and does not access email content outside the active Gmail thread.

Information Shared With Services

Automessage sends the email subject, message text, selected model, and tone preference to an Automessage Cloudflare Worker proxy. The proxy forwards the necessary prompt content to OpenRouter so an AI model can generate reply suggestions.

Email content passes through the Cloudflare Worker proxy in transit. The proxy is designed not to store email content. OpenRouter and its model providers may process submitted content according to their own policies.

Local Storage

Automessage stores only extension settings locally in Chrome, such as selected AI model and tone preference. Reply suggestions may be cached temporarily in extension memory for performance and are not permanently stored by the extension.

Analytics And Tracking

Automessage does not include analytics, advertising trackers, telemetry, or behavior tracking.

Data Sale Or Transfer

Automessage does not sell, rent, or share personal data for advertising or unrelated purposes.

Security

Automessage uses HTTPS for network requests. The OpenRouter API key is stored only in the Cloudflare Worker environment and is not included in the Chrome extension.

Your Choices

You can stop all extension processing by disabling or uninstalling Automessage. You can also clear locally stored extension settings by removing the extension from Chrome.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact the developer through the Automessage Chrome Web Store listing or GitHub profile: github.com/epaultex-svg.
